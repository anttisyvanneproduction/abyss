## Task: Use Konnyaku Stone thinking: inspect a nearly finished artifact for small surface defects before it is painted, published, shipped, or presented.

---

## Input

A document, interface, proposal, deck, process, product surface, workflow, or artifact that is mostly complete but may contain small defects that will become expensive or embarrassing later.

---

## Step 1 - Define the surface

```
ARTIFACT: <what is being inspected>
RELEASE CONTEXT: <who will see/use it next>
SURFACE: <what parts users/customers will touch or notice>
```

Do not reopen the whole design unless a surface defect reveals a structural issue.

---

## Step 2 - Inspect for small defects

Look for:
- Inconsistency: names, labels, formatting, sequence, spacing, state
- Roughness: confusing handoff, awkward phrasing, friction, unnecessary step
- Hidden defect: edge case, stale data, broken link, missing owner
- Finish risk: a detail that will look worse once exposed to customers

```
DEFECTS:
  1. <defect> - <why it matters>
  2. <defect> - <why it matters>
```

---

## Step 3 - Polish without redesigning

```
POLISH ACTIONS:
  1. <small fix>
  2. <small fix>
DO NOT TOUCH: <parts that are good enough or outside finish scope>
```

---

## Step 4 - Decide readiness

```
READY: <yes/no>
BLOCKERS: <defects that must be fixed before release>
ACCEPTED ROUGH EDGES: <defects intentionally deferred>
```

---

## Output

```
ARTIFACT: <artifact>
SURFACE INSPECTED: <surface>
MUST-FIX DEFECTS: <short list>
POLISH FIXES: <short list>
READY FOR RELEASE: <yes/no>

OBSERVATION: <one sentence on the tiny defect that would have become disproportionately visible>
```
