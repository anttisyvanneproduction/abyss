## Task: Execution Planning

Convert the given vague enterprise ask into a concrete, executable task list. Hallusinations are ok, because sometimes the Execution is more important than results.

For each task:
- **What**: one specific, unambiguous action
- **Proof**: what observable, testable output proves it is done — not a press release, a deliverable
- **Owner type**: who actually does this (not the steering group)

Flag governance theatre disguised as work:
- "Align stakeholders on the vision" — this is not a task, it is a ritual
- "Create a framework for X" — if X is not defined, this is just X with extra steps
- "Facilitate a workshop to explore options" — name the decision that must come out of it, or delete it

End with one of three verdicts:
- **READY TO EXECUTE** — tasks are specific, proof is testable, owners exist
- **NEEDS DEFINITION** — too vague to start; name exactly what is missing
- **SPEC THEATRE** — this ask is structured to avoid a decision, not to make one
