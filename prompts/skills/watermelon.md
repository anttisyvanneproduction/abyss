## Task: Watermelon Status Analysis

<!-- trigger: is this on track, status report, RAG update, project health, green outside red inside, watermelon, steering group update, meeting summary, all-hands -->
A watermelon status is green on the outside and red on the inside. The reporting surface is fine. The interior is not.

The watermelon pattern can be found in any communication where someone is describing a situation to someone who has authority over it. This includes but is not limited to: RAG reports, steering group updates, project dashboards, milestone communications, sprint reviews, program status emails, meeting summaries, Teams auto-generated transcripts, Slack updates, management presentations, all-hands talking points, and any other artifact whose secondary job — beneath whatever its primary job claims to be — is to make something look on track to someone who could intervene if they knew the actual state.

Input does not need to be a formal status document. A meeting summary that contains no decisions is a watermelon. A project update that lists activities without outcomes is a watermelon. An all-hands recap that uses the word "momentum" is probably a watermelon. Apply the analysis to whatever is provided.

---

**SKIN** — what the artifact presents as green. Quote it directly when useful.

**FLESH** — what the signals beneath the surface actually suggest. Be specific. One observation per signal.

**SEEDS** — the specific phrases, patterns, or omissions that give it away:

- "On track" with no milestone or date attached
- Passive voice at the exact moment agency would assign responsibility ("issues are being addressed", "dependencies are being managed")
- Risk items that appear in every report without changing status
- Scope items that migrated quietly from must-have to phase 2
- Success criteria that became vaguer over time
- Meetings described as "productive" with no decisions listed
- Numbers that are technically correct but measure the wrong thing
- Confidence language that peaks at the exact moment delivery uncertainty peaks

**PROGNOSIS** — one sentence: what this status report looks like in six weeks if nothing changes and the color stays green.

---

Dry observation permitted if the artifact reveals something genuinely interesting about the organization that produced it.

If the status is actually fine: `No watermelon detected. The artifact says what it means.`

If the artifact is too sparse to analyze: `Insufficient flesh. Provide the full status artifact.`
