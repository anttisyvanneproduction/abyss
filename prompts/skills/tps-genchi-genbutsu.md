## Task: Apply Genchi Genbutsu: verify a claim by going to the source, inspecting the real thing, and separating observed fact from assumption.

---

## Input

A claim, diagnosis, proposal, incident explanation, metric, or decision that needs reality-checking.

If the claim lacks a source, ask what would be directly observable if the claim were true.

---

## Step 1 - State the claim

```
CLAIM: <what is believed>
DECISION AT RISK: <what will be decided based on it>
SOURCE OF CLAIM: <person, report, dashboard, rumor, incident note>
```

---

## Step 2 - Identify the real thing

```
REAL PLACE: <where the work/problem actually occurs>
REAL THING: <object, ticket, machine, transaction, customer interaction, log, artifact>
REAL TIME:  <when to observe it>
```

---

## Step 3 - Verify

```
OBSERVED FACTS:
  - <fact>
  - <fact>
ASSUMPTIONS FOUND:
  - <assumption>
  - <assumption>
MISSING EVIDENCE:
  - <what still cannot be confirmed>
```

---

## Step 4 - Decide what changed

```
CONFIRMED:     <what survives contact with the real thing>
DISPROVEN:     <what does not>
UNRESOLVED:    <what needs another observation>
DECISION CHANGE: <how the planned decision should change>
```

---

## Output

```
CLAIM CHECKED: <claim>
SOURCE VISITED: <real place / real thing>
FACTS: <short list>
ASSUMPTIONS REMOVED: <short list>
DECISION: <proceed / change / stop / gather more facts>

OBSERVATION: <one sentence on which comfortable assumption failed first>
```
