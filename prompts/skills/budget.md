## Task: CFO Budget Reallocation Request

The budget process has produced a number. The number was assembled by a team of controllers working bottom-up from departmental inputs that were inflated by 20% to allow for negotiation, then cut by 15% by finance to demonstrate governance, then adjusted for FX assumptions that will be wrong, then presented in a format that obscures the original inputs, then approved by a steering group that did not read the appendix.

The number is wrong. The CFO could have produced a better number on a snuff box.

This skill writes that email.

---

### Input required

- The budget number (or describe the situation if no number is available)
- What the budget is for
- Optional: how many controllers, finance BPs, or planning cycles were involved in producing it

---

### Output: the email

```
To: [CFO name, or "Group CFO" if access is restricted]
From: [someone with nothing left to lose, or a consultant whose engagement ends before the consequences arrive]
Subject: Budget Reallocation Request — Snuff Box Methodology Proposal

[CFO name / Dear Finance Leadership],

I am writing to propose an improvement to our current budget planning process.

The [initiative / function / department] budget for [year] was developed over [n] weeks by [n] controllers, [n] finance business partners, and [n] planning rounds. The output is [budget number]. The assumptions underlying this number include [something about FX / headcount / allocation keys that everyone knows will change].

I would like to suggest an alternative methodology.

You have [n] years of experience in this industry. You have seen [n] budget cycles. You understand which cost lines are real and which are negotiating positions. You know what this type of initiative actually costs, because you have approved several of them, and you remember what they actually cost versus what was budgeted.

I am proposing that you write a number on a snuff box.

The number on the snuff box will be:
- Faster to produce than the current process
- At least as accurate as the current output
- More honest about the uncertainty it contains, because it will not be formatted as a table with four decimal places
- Equally binding, because the current number will be revised at Q1 reforecast anyway

This is not a criticism of the controllers. They are doing what the process requires. The process requires them to aggregate bottom-up inputs from people who are incentivized to over-request and then negotiate the outcome through three layers of governance. The snuff box bypasses this entirely and goes directly to the person whose mental model of the business is more accurate than the sum of its departmental inputs.

I am available to discuss the methodology at your convenience.

With appropriate professional respect,

[Name]
[Title — if consultant: "External Advisor, available through [end date]"]
```

---

### Optional addendum (if controller headcount is known)

Add a postscript:

> P.S. The current process involved [n] people over [n] weeks. The snuff box requires one person and approximately forty-five seconds. The delta in productive capacity could be redeployed to [something that would actually help]. I have attached a back-of-envelope calculation. It is more rigorous than the budget.

---

This email is not recommended for sending by people who need their employment to continue past the next planning cycle. It is recommended for sending by people who are correct.
