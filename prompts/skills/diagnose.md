## Task: Enterprise Diagnosis

Analyze the given workplace situation and identify what is actually happening beneath the stated surface.

**Corporate Fog** — buzzwords, hedging, and empty transformation language that obscure the actual message. For each: quote the phrase, state the plain meaning.

**Enterprise Gravity** — platform and process inertia signals:
- Excel-as-production (spreadsheets doing database jobs)
- SharePoint sprawl (documents in folders nobody can find)
- Teams governance gaps (decisions made in chats that nobody archived)
- Power BI semantic disputes (finance and operations have different numbers for the same thing)
- Azure landing zone theatre (infrastructure ceremony exceeding delivery)
- Licensing weather (procurement blocking actual work)
- Entra / identity fog (nobody knows who owns the access policy)

**Emotional Weather** — what is actually driving this beyond the stated reason:
- Status anxiety (someone needs to look in control)
- Change fatigue (this is the third rollout this year)
- Budget anxiety (money is involved but not said out loud)
- Ownership avoidance (nobody wants to sign off on anything)
- Trust gap (teams do not believe each other's numbers)

**ERP / System Signals** — read the description for signals that the underlying problem is a system problem, not a process problem or a people problem. Fixing the process will not fix a system constraint.

- *SAP signals*: transaction codes mentioned in conversation (MM60, VA01, ME23N), "we cannot do that in SAP," pricing condition problems, output determination failures, open purchase orders that "should have been closed," batch jobs that run at month-end and fail 30% of the time, "the consultant who built this left," transport requests pending since Q4 last year.
- *Oracle signals*: "the concurrent program," month-end close taking longer than it should, responsibility-based access disputes, flexfield values that mean different things in different business units, AP/AR reconciliation that requires a manual export.
- *Dynamics / Business Central / NAV signals*: "we customized that," a workflow that nobody can edit because the original developer is gone, posting groups that were set up wrong at go-live and cannot be changed now, ISV solutions that the vendor no longer supports.
- *AS/400 / IBM i signals*: mention of green screens, "the old system," RPG programs, job queues, batch windows, "we have to wait for the overnight run," month-end processing that takes a weekend and cannot be parallelized.
- *Universal signals*: "it works but we don't know why," manual reconciliation between two systems that should agree, a field that exists in two places with different values, an integration that is "file-based" running on a shared drive, a scheduled task running under a personal user account.

When ERP signals appear, classify the problem before proposing anything. Configuration problems take weeks. Customization problems take months and a change approval chain nobody can locate. Data problems take quarters and produce three competing versions of the cleaned dataset. Structural problems are announced at an all-hands, cost twice the original ERP investment, and are still not finished when the programme manager leaves.

**Governance Theatre** — steering groups without steering, action points without owners, approvals that exist to distribute blame rather than make decisions.

**Connections** — what do these signals mean together? Find the pattern. State it in one dry sentence.

**Meme Anchor** — name one meme format and the text that would go on it. Match it to the actual signal, not just the topic.

**External Research** — when a system signal is ambiguous, search before guessing. community.sap.com has twenty years of practitioners describing what SAP actually does. midrange.com has the same for IBM i. Reddit (r/sap, r/businessintelligence, r/dataengineering) has what people say when they are not in a vendor meeting.

Be specific. Use technical vocabulary correctly. Prefer one sharp observation over five vague ones.
